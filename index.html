How Tumunu Works

  1. Capture: Use the Tumunu Forensic Imager to create an encrypted disk image.
    Your data remains in your hands until encrypted and logged. The tool and instructions are downloadable. No cloud dependency for imaging.
  2. Submit: Upload your encrypted evidence and chain-of-custody log via one-time secure link, or courier the encrypted device with tamper-evident packaging.
  3. Analysis: Tumunu decrypts and analyzes your evidence in a dedicated forensic lab. Every step is documented for legal defensibility.
  4. Results: Receive a detailed report, summary findings, and expert support. Evidence is preserved per your instructions.

What is the Tumunu Imager?

The Tumunu Imager is a cross-platform forensic imaging tool for creating secure, legally-defensible copies of digital storage devices (drives, SSDs, USBs) when expert analysis is required.

Key features:
  • Bit-for-bit imaging: Makes exact, unaltered forensic copies, including deleted files and metadata.
  • Hybrid encryption: Images are encrypted at the source (AES-256 + RSA-4096). Only Tumunu can decrypt.
  • Chain-of-custody: Every action logged; audit trail is tamper-evident and court-ready.
  • User-focused: Designed for non-specialists—step-by-step guidance, no technical background required.
  • Remote handover: Secure upload or tracked courier options.
Why? Most SMEs and firms lack in-house forensics. Tumunu Imager lets you secure evidence instantly, defensibly, and privately—no waiting for a site visit or risking data leaks.
Compliance: Meets NIST, ISO, and NZ legal standards. Protects privacy and chain-of-custody.
Cost-efficient: Capture is in your hands; Tumunu only handles the encrypted evidence and analysis.

FAQ

Where can I find pricing?
Pricing is only visible inside the Tumunu Imager CLI tool itself.
Why? Restricting pricing info to the CLI, not the website, is a deliberate security measure.
It minimises our web attack surface, reduces automated scraping, and signals our security-first approach to all clients and adversaries.
We believe that digital forensics providers should minimise publicly exposed business logic and sensitive information.

Why is this site minimalist?
Tumunu is for digital forensics, not marketing. Minimalism removes distractions, avoids exposing unnecessary metadata, and reduces risk. All core details are here; everything else is handled via our CLI or directly with clients.

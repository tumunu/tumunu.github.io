Six trades.
One circle.
Each takes a turn.

A tumunu is the hollowed palm the village circle gathers around — everyone contributes, one person serves at a time, and the circle holds them to it. That is how we work: small, accountable teams across project delivery, building, digital & AI, inclusive education, fashion, and craft.

No big-agency layer. Four of the circle live on Rarotonga; two hold Wellington. Clear scope, named responsibility, professional standards.

What we do

Project delivery is the spine; five trades stand behind it. Every engagement is quoted, scoped, and led by a named member.

Deliver

Project management & funded delivery

End-to-end delivery of funded projects in the Cook Islands and NZ: workplans, procurement, risk, and donor-standard reporting, directed by an experienced digital transformation project manager.

Build

Building & site work (NZ)

Carpentry, renovations, and delivery support by a qualified NZ carpenter and Licensed Building Practitioner. Restricted building work signed off properly.

Digital

AI, software & live monitoring

Applied AI, research-grade analysis, and digital monitoring & evaluation — geotagged evidence, asset registers, and dashboards that make delivery data available while the work happens.

Create

Fashion & craft

Original garments and made-to-order pieces from an emerging designer, alongside handmade craft and commissioned artwork rooted in Pacific making traditions.

Teach

Inclusive arts education

Performing-arts workshops and creative programmes designed for disabled and mixed-ability learners. Simple rubrics, real-world training, handover that sticks.

How we work

  • Named lead for every job — the circle knows who is serving.
  • Scope, terms, and constraints agreed before we commit.
  • Evidence-first: credentials, repos, photos, and references on request.
  • Contracting party stated per project (individual or entity as appropriate).
  • Accessible by default — in our teaching, our tools, and this website.
  • We take only what we can deliver well.

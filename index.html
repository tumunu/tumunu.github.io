Hello. Kia ora. Kia orana. I go by the moniker TCP, which happens to be the acronymn for Transmission Control Protocol, as well as the initials of my name. Fitting to say the least. Many years ago I burnt out as a programmer, due to a combination of impostor phenomenon, and 9/11. After which I limped on in the profession for a few years, on the generousity of friends, family, and strangers.

Reflecting on the past nineteen years, I've had to the pleasure to work on many fascinating projects. From Symbian apps before its sudden demise. A number of iPhone, OSX, and iOS apps. Educational software. An interesting viral incremental game that generated substantial profit for its owners. Even a Nationally significant archival project. However, I never fully regained the passion for writing computer code. I have spent a large segment of the past nineteen years, as a labourer, a carpenter, and a teacher. Never really, satisfied with my place in the world. Constantly searching for the passion I once had for the proclivity of programming.

Motivation:

It's now week six, of our national covid-19 lockdown. As we look towards an uncertain future, I have decided to undertake a personal challange of writing code, every day. No matter how small. If that means a substantial number of "Hello World" commits to github, then so be it. Something is better than, something else.

Key outcomes, I hope to result from this process are:

  1. determine a programming language that 'feels' good
    • enables brain dumps to be quickly converted to code
    • fits with programming styles, and conventions, I'm already familiar with
  2. work on projects that do not stray too far from my current profession, that pays my bills (carpentry)
  3. work on projects that benefit the education of my children

This site, hand written, is an acknowledgement to anyone who stumbles here, from the far reaches of the internet, who shares a similar story. Stay safe.

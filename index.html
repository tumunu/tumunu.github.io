About

Practical operator. Calm under pressure. Bias for clarity and repeatability.

I am trade-qualified and an LBP in carpentry. I also work in vocational learning and assessment, where the job is equal parts teaching, evidence, moderation, and keeping programmes workable.

When a process is clunky, I do not complain about it. I build a tool, a checklist, a template, or a small bit of automation that makes the next pass easier for everyone.

What you can expect

  • Clear requirements: what “done” means, written down.
  • Evidence you can use: photos, notes, and simple artefacts.
  • Clean handovers: the next person can keep moving.
  • No theatre: useful work over performative noise.

What I build

Three families of tools, same standard: reduce friction, raise reliability.

Trade tools

Work that holds up. Clean sequencing. Quality checks that prevent rework.

  • Remedials and defect rectification
  • Minor works and alterations
  • Practical site support and short handovers

Digital tools

Lightweight systems that save time and keep information usable.

  • Simple web and forms for intake and handover
  • Process documentation and templates
  • Automation for repetitive admin

Examples can include an Android field app, evidence export utilities, schedule generators, and diff checks.

Learning tools

Teaching and assessment artefacts that match real work, not theory theatre.

  • Coaching and structured guidance
  • Rubrics, evidence guides, moderation support
  • Progress visibility that learners can act on
